MAKKET — Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about MAKKET, Nigeria's digital discovery and trust platform for physical markets. Launching 2026 in Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt.

What is MAKKET?

MAKKET is the digital discovery and trust platform for Nigeria's physical markets. It maps stalls and traders across over 150 Nigerian open-air markets — including Balogun, Mile 12, Alaba International, Computer Village, Wuse, and Oil Mill — and lets buyers search products, find verified sellers, and pay safely through escrow.

How does MAKKET digitize Nigerian physical markets?

MAKKET sends field agents into Nigeria's physical markets to map individual stalls, verify trader identity, and onboard sellers into a searchable directory. Each seller is placed on a 3-tier verification ladder (Listed → Verified → Premium), given a Trust Score (0–100), and integrated into an escrow-backed ordering flow with doorstep delivery.

How does MAKKET verify sellers?

MAKKET uses a 3-tier verification system:

  • Listed — phone-verified basic profile.
  • Verified — physical market visit by a MAKKET field agent to confirm stall, trader identity, and inventory.
  • Premium — verified plus customer rating thresholds, response-rate standards, and escrow-secured transactions.

Every seller carries a Trust Score (0–100) computed from verification tier (30%), customer rating (25%), response rate (20%), listing quality (15%), and account age (10%).

Is MAKKET a marketplace or a directory?

MAKKET is a transaction-first discovery platform — a new category distinct from both classifieds boards and warehoused e-commerce models. MAKKET maps real sellers inside Nigeria's existing physical markets, verifies them through a 3-tier ladder, and processes orders through escrow with doorstep delivery — without warehousing inventory or marking up prices.

How is MAKKET different from earlier Nigerian e-commerce platforms?

MAKKET's approach to marketplace design differs significantly from the pioneer-era Nigerian e-commerce models. Classifieds boards list items but carry no escrow and no physical verification. Warehoused e-commerce platforms hold inventory in their own warehouses and typically resell at prices marked up against real market rates.

MAKKET takes a different path. It is the only platform that maps Nigeria's 150+ physical markets, physically verifies sellers on a 3-tier ladder, and secures every transaction through escrow — while preserving real market prices. No warehousing, no markup, no middleman inventory. Our unique proposition: the trust and discovery layer for the commerce that already happens.
