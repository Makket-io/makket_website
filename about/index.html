The problem

Roughly 80% of commerce in Nigeria happens in physical markets — Balogun, Mile 12, Alaba International, Computer Village, Wuse, Oil Mill, and over 300 others. None of these markets are searchable online. None carry trust signals a first-time buyer can verify. Buyers waste 2–4 hours per shopping trip walking the stalls, negotiating, and risking scams. Sellers can only be found by people who already know them.

Our approach

MAKKET is the digital layer that fixes this. We are building a new category of platform — not a classifieds board, not a warehoused e-commerce site, but a transaction-first discovery platform that maps real sellers inside Nigeria's existing physical markets.

Our approach differs significantly from the pioneer-era Nigerian e-commerce models. We don't warehouse inventory. We don't mark up prices. We connect buyers directly to the stall-owning traders who already serve millions of Nigerians every day — and we add the trust, verification, and payment security that the market experience currently lacks.

How it works

  • Search — buyer types a product into MAKKET (e.g. "Samsung A54", "ankara fabric", "tomato basket").
  • Discover — MAKKET shows verified sellers across all relevant markets, sorted by price, distance, trust score, and rating.
  • Compare — buyer sees real market prices (no markup), seller verification tier, response time, and ratings.
  • Order — buyer places the order through MAKKET. Payment is held in escrow.
  • Deliver — MAKKET dispatches the order to the buyer's door (or buyer can arrange pickup).

Trust and verification

Every seller on MAKKET goes through a 3-tier verification process:

  • Listed — basic profile with phone verification.
  • Verified — physical market visit by MAKKET field agents to confirm stall existence, trader identity, and inventory.
  • Premium — verified plus customer rating thresholds, response-rate standards, and escrow-secured transactions.

Each seller carries a Trust Score (0–100) computed from verification tier, customer rating, response rate, listing quality, and account age.

Where we operate

MAKKET launches in 2026 in three cities:

  • Lagos — Balogun Market, Mile 12, Alaba International, Computer Village (Ikeja), Ladipo, Trade Fair Complex
  • Abuja — Wuse, Utako, Garki
  • Port Harcourt — Oil Mill, Creek Road, Mile 1

Post-launch expansion: Enugu, Ibadan, Abeokuta, Kano, Kaduna, Aba (Ariaria International), Onitsha, Owerri, Warri, Benin City, Asaba.
